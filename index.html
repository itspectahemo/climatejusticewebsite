Women of Color
in Climate Justice

In the global dialogue on climate change, there exists a profound silence—the voices of women of color who are simultaneously the most vulnerable to climate impacts and the most innovative in creating solutions.

Climate change is not just an environmental issue—it's a story of survival, resilience, and systemic inequality.
Women of color are:

Disproportionately affected by climate disasters

Often living in regions most vulnerable to environmental changes

Carrying the burden of community adaptation with minimal representation in decision-making spaces
